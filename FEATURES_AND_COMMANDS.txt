================================================================================
QUANT TOOLKIT - FEATURES AND COMMANDS
================================================================================

Research only. Not investment advice. No live brokerage trading is performed.

Run commands from the project root:

  python3 cli.py <command> [options]

Data:
  - Prices: Yahoo Finance
  - Filing-risk scores: cached in tenk_cache.json
  - Default portfolio universe: semis
  - Default portfolio momentum preset: mom_10d
  - Default headline transaction cost: 10 bps round-trip


================================================================================
1. BEST DEFAULT: COMPREHENSIVE UNIVERSE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Use this when you want the broadest single report for any universe:

  python3 cli.py portfolio compare --universe <universe_name> --years 10 --momentum-preset mom_10d --cost-bps 10 --validate

Semis example:

  python3 cli.py portfolio compare --universe semis --years 10 --momentum-preset mom_10d --cost-bps 10 --validate

Custom ticker example:

  python3 cli.py portfolio compare --tickers AMAT,ASX,COHR,INTC,LRCX,GFS,ARM,AMD,MRVL,ALAB --years 10 --momentum-preset mom_10d --cost-bps 10 --validate

This command is the most complete one-stop analysis. It includes:
  - Data quality and coverage checks
  - Strategy comparison:
      1. equal-weight benchmark
      2. cross-sectional momentum long-only
      3. cross-sectional momentum long/short
      4. combined signal long-only
  - Current portfolio ranking and combined signal report
  - Single-stock mean-reversion and momentum confirmation
  - Cached annual filing risk: 10-K or 20-F
  - Cached quarterly filing risk: 10-Q, where available
  - Foreign issuer filing labels such as "20-F only (no 10-Q)"
  - Risk contribution and covariance-based sizing review
  - Walk-forward out-of-sample validation
  - Equal-weight benchmark comparison
  - Transaction-cost sensitivity at multiple bps levels
  - Final gated conclusion

Important:
  - The command reads existing filing scores from tenk_cache.json.
  - It does not refresh SEC filings or call the LLM by itself.
  - Refresh filing scores separately with tenk_reader.py when needed.

Trend / vol-timing overlay (risk-adjusted timing, judged vs cash):

  python3 cli.py portfolio trend --universe semis --years 12 --trend-mode sma --cost-bps 10
  python3 cli.py portfolio trend --universe semis --years 12 --trend-mode tsmom --vol-target --target-vol 0.15

  - This is NOT a benchmark-relative strategy. Cross-sectional momentum matches
    the equal-weight basket; this command instead TIMES EXPOSURE to the basket
    to improve risk-adjusted return (Sharpe) and cut drawdowns.
  - --trend-mode sma   : hold the basket when its price is above its N-day moving
    average, else move to cash (N is walk-forward selected from --trend-windows).
  - --trend-mode tsmom : hold when the trailing N-day return is positive.
  - --vol-target scales exposure toward --target-vol using trailing realized vol
    (de-risks in turbulent regimes; --max-leverage caps exposure, default 1.0x).
  - --cash-rate sets the annual return earned when out of the market (default 0).
  - No look-ahead: exposure at day t uses data <= t and is applied to the next
    day's return; cost is charged on exposure changes.
  - The report compares the overlay OOS vs the ALWAYS-INVESTED basket and vs cash
    (Sharpe / AnnRet / Vol / MaxDD), shows % time invested, turnover, cost drag,
    fold consistency, and a verdict:
      IMPROVES RISK-ADJUSTED  - higher Sharpe AND no worse drawdown than buy&hold
      REDUCES DRAWDOWN ONLY   - lower return but materially smaller drawdowns
      NO IMPROVEMENT          - after costs, timing did not beat holding
  - Expect trend overlays to shine in bear/crash regimes and to lag in relentless
    bull markets; on a strong-trending basket they often cut drawdowns without
    raising Sharpe.

Peer-group-neutral construction (avoid betting on one sub-industry):

  python3 cli.py portfolio compare --universe semis --years 10 --momentum-preset mom_10d --score-mode residual_momentum --group-neutral --cost-bps 10

  - --group-neutral builds a LONG-ONLY book that splits capital EQUALLY across
    semiconductor peer groups (memory_storage, equipment, foundry, eda_ip,
    analog_power, ai_connectivity, osat_packaging, broad_semis), then picks the
    top names WITHIN each group by the chosen score_mode. This stops the book
    from becoming a concentrated bet on whichever sub-theme is hottest.
  - --group-top-frac F sets the fraction of names selected within each group
    (default: same as --top-frac; at least one name per group).
  - Thin groups (fewer than 2 of the active tickers) are merged into
    broad_semis so a singleton sub-industry cannot dominate.
  - The flag implies validation: the report shows the peer-group membership,
    the current group-neutral book by group, and a walk-forward OOS active-IR
    verdict for the group-neutral construction (no look-ahead).
  - Sizing-scheme and cost-sweep self-checks are skipped under --group-neutral
    because those diagnostics use the plain top-k book, not the group book.

Combined-signal out-of-sample validation:

  python3 cli.py portfolio compare --universe semis --years 10 --momentum-preset mom_10d --cost-bps 10 --validate-combined

  - --validate-combined implies --validate and adds a dedicated walk-forward
    report for the COMBINED long-only book (XS rank + z-score / momentum
    confirmation overlay), tuned per fold on the train window only.
  - It tunes combined params per fold: z_overextended in [1.0, 1.5, 2.0],
    z_oversold in [-0.5, -1.0, -1.5], require_momentum_buy in [True, False],
    and score_mode in [raw_momentum, relative_momentum, residual_momentum]
    (54 combos/fold), selecting on active IR vs the equal-weight benchmark.
  - Output compares three books on the SAME OOS folds: pure XS long-only,
    combined long-only, and the equal-weight benchmark (OOS Sharpe, AnnRet,
    MaxDD, active IR), plus the combined active-IR CI, selection-adjusted null
    bar, benchmark correlation, and an EDGE / MATCHES BENCHMARK / FAILS verdict.
  - The combined book is only credited with EDGE if it beats the benchmark
    OOS after costs and clears the selection-adjusted bar — never on absolute
    return alone. Gating/confirmation usually trades breadth for lower turnover,
    not higher active IR.

================================================================================
5. MODEL SUMMARY
================================================================================

Cross-sectional momentum:
  Ranks stocks relative to the selected universe. Longs are top-ranked names;
  shorts or avoids are bottom-ranked names, depending on command mode.

Single-stock momentum:
  Confirms whether a ticker has positive time-series momentum on its own.

Mean reversion:
  Uses z-score versus a rolling mean to detect stretched or oversold conditions.

Combined signal:
  Merges cross-sectional rank, single-stock momentum, and mean-reversion state
  into BUY, WAIT, WATCH, AVOID, or SHORT_CANDIDATE.

Filing-risk overlay:
  Uses cached annual 10-K/20-F and quarterly 10-Q scores. A BUY can be flagged
  with risk conflict if filing language worsened beyond the threshold.

Risk sizing:
  Estimates covariance on coverage-approved names and reports equal-weight risk
  contribution plus alternative sizing methods such as risk parity.

Validation:
  Walk-forward validation checks whether the strategy has out-of-sample edge
  versus an equal-weight benchmark after realistic transaction costs.

9. TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

Unknown universe:
  Run `python3 cli.py portfolio universes`.

Invalid command option:
  Use `--cost-bps 10`, not `--10bps`.

No filing data:
  Refresh tenk_cache.json with tenk_reader.py. Foreign issuers may only have
  20-F annual filings and no 10-Q quarterly filings.

Stale filing score:
  Re-run tenk_reader.py with --force.

Insufficient history:
  Use --years 10 for validation-heavy work. Young tickers may be shown as
  insufficient history and excluded from validation or risk sizing.

Yahoo Finance errors:
  Check ticker spelling, network access, and rate limits.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 8 — POINT-IN-TIME FILING-RISK FACTOR (portfolio filing)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Turns cached 10-K/20-F risk-language change scores (tenk_cache.json) into a
point-in-time ticker x date panel and uses it as a NEGATIVE filter on the
long-only book (never as a buy signal).

No look-ahead:
  - A filing scored on filing_date becomes active on filing_date + 1 trading day
    (configurable via --filing-activation-lag) and persists until a newer filing
    for that ticker supersedes it. Dates before the first filing are "no info".

Filter actions (names whose ACTIVE score <= threshold):
  - exclude     : drop the position, then renormalize the long book (pure tilt).
  - half_weight : halve the position, then renormalize.

Cadence (annual vs quarterly):
  10-Ks/20-Fs are ANNUAL -> ~1 score/name/yr, so an annual-only cache needs many
  years to be testable. tenk_reader.py also scores 10-Q QUARTERLY changes
  (seasonality-controlled, year-over-year same quarter) -> ~4 scores/name/yr.
  Both write into the SAME cache (tenk_cache.json) with a `source` field, and
  the factor ingests both automatically: quarterly scores simply produce more
  frequent step-changes in the point-in-time panel. The report shows the cadence
  mix and median update spacing. To densify:
    python3 tenk_reader.py --tickers <T1,T2,...> --filing-type both

Data-sufficiency gate (the important part):
  Because updates are infrequent, a thin cache CANNOT validate the factor. The
  report ALWAYS prints sufficiency first and only runs walk-forward validation
  when the cache clears the bar (>= 4 names with >= 4 scored updates each, >= 3y
  span, >= 20 total updates). The gate is cadence-aware: quarterly scoring
  reaches the per-name update count in far less calendar time than annual.
  Otherwise it prints "INSUFFICIENT — validation SKIPPED" and explains what
  backfill is needed. (The current cache is a recent snapshot, so it correctly
  reports INSUFFICIENT.)

Commands:
  # Sufficiency check + (if data allows) base-vs-filtered active-IR validation
  python3 cli.py portfolio filing --universe semis --years 10 \
      --momentum-preset mom_10d --cost-bps 10

  # Tune the filter
  python3 cli.py portfolio filing --universe semis --years 10 \
      --momentum-preset mom_10d --filing-threshold -0.30 \
      --filing-action half_weight --filing-activation-lag 1 --cost-bps 10

To make it validatable: backfill annual filing-change scores per name across
many years (EDGAR holds the raw 10-K/20-F filings back decades; tenk_reader.py
fetches + scores them). Until then the factor is infrastructure-ready but
under-powered by design.
